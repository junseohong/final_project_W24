Bukchon Hanok Village

Nestled between Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung palaces, Bukchon Hanok Village is home to hundreds of traditional houses called 'hanok' that date back to the Joseon Dynasty. Today, many of these hanoks operate as cultural centers, guesthouses, restaurants, and tea houses, providing visitors a glimpse into the traditional Korean lifestyle.

Myeongdong Shopping District

A bustling area known for its vibrant shopping scene, Myeongdong is one of Seoul's major retail centers featuring international brands, local boutiques, and high-end Korean cosmetics stores. The area is also famous for its street food stalls that offer delicious snacks like tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes) and hotteok (sweet pancakes).

Insadong

This neighborhood is the go-to place for traditional Korean culture and crafts. Insadong's streets are lined with antique shops, art galleries, ceramic stores, and traditional tea houses. It's an excellent spot for buying unique Korean souvenirs such as handmade hanji (traditional Korean paper), traditional teas, or pottery.

N Seoul Tower

Located on Namsan Mountain in the center of the city, N Seoul Tower is not only a great vantage point to see panoramic views of Seoul, but it's also a cultural space with exhibitions, restaurants, and cafes. The area around the tower features a popular lock of love fence where couples leave padlocks as a symbol of their love.

Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP)

A major urban development landmark in Seoul, DDP is a blend of traditional markets, refurbished buildings, futuristic architecture, and cultural spaces. Designed by the renowned architect Zaha Hadid, this iconic site hosts international conferences, fashion shows, exhibitions, and other cultural events.
